### **Updated Vibe and UX Specification**

#### **Vibe: Impending Storm & Survival Gloom**

**Gathering Phase (The Warning Window):**

* **Visuals:** Heavy, overcast skies with a desaturated color palette of deep grays, muted campus greens, and dark blues. The lighting feels like the eerie, dim hours right before a hurricane makes landfall—shadows are soft but oppressive.  
* **Atmosphere:** High tension but physically calm. The feeling is "the clock is ticking."

**Survival Phase (The Impact):**

* **Visuals:** Noticeably darker and actively deteriorating. Locations show visible wind damage, scattered debris, and rising water levels. The color palette shifts to harsh, cold tones (blacks, storm-surge blues, and emergency indicator reds).  
* **Atmosphere:** Chaotic, claustrophobic, and hostile. The environment itself is now the enemy.

#### **User Experience (UX)**

**Gathering Phase UI:**

* Clean, functional, and urgency-driven.  
* Timers (real-world or in-game hours until landfall) and weather warnings are prominent but stable.  
* The campus map is fully visible but shows creeping hazard zones.  
* Scavenging actions feel calculated and organized.

**Survival Phase UI:**

* The UI reflects the physical degradation. UI elements might flicker slightly, and low-elevation map routes become visually blocked by "flooded" overlays.  
* The "Emergency Oracle" chat shifts tone from providing preparation checklists to issuing urgent, survival-critical directives based on your immediate location.  
* Resource bars (Health, Battery, Water) become the absolute focal point, with visual alerts (pulsing red) when critical thresholds are breached.  
* The lower the health, a vignette is stronger and lowers your visibility.
